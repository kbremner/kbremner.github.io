--- layout: default title: "About - Kyle Bremner | Software Engineer" published: true ---

Originally from Aberdeen, I came to Glasgow in 2008 to study for a Master of Engineering degree in Electronics & Software Engineering at the University of Glasgow. After graduating in May 2013 with a first class honours, I started work at Ecebs, developing mobile application utilising NFC technology as part of innovative smart ticketing solutions.

I have worked on everything from mobile applications utilising NFC, to a high-speed dynamometer logging system, to a Grails-powered API backend and mobile application for a network of energy-harvesting sensor nodes. I enjoy keeping my experience varied.

I also try to keep in touch with the thriving tech community in Glasgow. I regularly attend Tech Meetup Glasgow, and have recently taken part in the Glasgow Startup Weekend, as well as the Open Glasgow Public Safety Hackathon. These events are a great opportunity to network with other developers and discover what else is going on in Glasgow, one that I try to take full advantage of.

In my spare time, I play pipes for Milngavie Pipe Band. The band was recently promoted to grade 3A and hopes to do well in the 2014 competing season.

Whilst I was studying for my degree, I was also part of the Glasgow & Strathclyde Universities Officer Training Corps Pipes & Drums, reaching the rank of Junior Under Officer (JUO) and acting Pipe Major before leaving on completion of my degree.
